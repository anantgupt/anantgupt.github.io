I work with Prof. Upamanyu Madhow in the WCSL lab at UCSB . My research aims at developing sensing and inference frameworks for low cost millimeter wave systems. I have worked in industry (Qualcomm, National Instruments ) on algorithm design and development projects for wireless technologies such as 5G mmW, 802.11x WLAN, NFC, GPS. My interest lie at streamlining signal processing frameworks for sensing in future autonomous applications. I received MS from UCSB in 2016 and BTech+MTech in ECE from IIT Kharagpur in 2013.

HFH 4164A, UCSB, CA-93106 - anantgupta@ucsb.edu

Research Projects: mm-wave sensing

  • Multi-objective optimization to construct large-effective aperture antennas using a sparse array of subarray architecture.
  • Enhanced accuracy and Super-Resolution algorithms for 2D FMCW radar systems.
  • Strategic array design optimizing sensing coverage.
